<p>To connect Sendcloud to Shopify, you have two routes: the direct Sendcloud app for Shopify, or an order processing system like ShopLinkr that pulls in your Shopify orders and creates the labels through Sendcloud. The direct connection works fine when Shopify is your only channel. If you also sell on bol, Kaufland, or WooCommerce, you'll want to process everything in one place instead of switching per channel. We'll cover both options below.</p>

<h2>The direct Sendcloud app for Shopify</h2>
<p>Sendcloud is a shipping platform that lets you connect carriers like PostNL, DPD, and DHL to your webshop and create shipping labels with a few clicks. The fastest way to get started:</p>
<ol>
<li>Create a Sendcloud account and set up your carriers and shipping methods there.</li>
<li>Open the Shopify App Store and search for the official Sendcloud app.</li>
<li>Install the app and give Sendcloud permission to read your orders. Make sure you have enough permissions in Shopify to install apps.</li>
<li>Check in Sendcloud that new Shopify orders are coming through and create a test label.</li>
</ol>
<p>From then on, you create labels in Sendcloud and the platform sends the track &amp; trace back to Shopify, so your customer automatically gets a shipping confirmation.</p>

ShopLinkr pulls your <a href="/en/features/orders">orders</a> from Shopify (and from bol, WooCommerce, and Kaufland) and bundles them in one workspace. You process an order, pick the carrier, and ShopLinkr creates the Sendcloud label. The track &amp; trace goes back to the right sales channel automatically.</p>
<p>Setting up the connection works like this:</p>

<li>Connect Sendcloud as a <a href="/en/features/carriers">carrier</a> in ShopLinkr and choose your shipping methods.</li>
