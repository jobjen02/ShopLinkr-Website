<p>A free inventory management Excel template is the fastest way to get a grip on what you have in stock, what needs reordering, and how much you can still sell. All you need is Excel or Google Sheets. Below you'll find the columns and formulas that actually matter, how to fill in the template, and the point where a spreadsheet starts working against you.</p>

<h2>Which columns does a good template need?</h2>
<p>Keep it simple. A workable inventory template fits on a single tab. These columns form the foundation:</p>
<ul>
<li><p><strong>SKU</strong> and <strong>product name</strong>, so every item is uniquely identifiable</p></li>
<li><p><strong>Current stock</strong>, the quantity physically on the shelf</p></li>
<li><p><strong>Minimum stock</strong>, the threshold below which you need to reorder</p></li>
<li><p><strong>Cost price</strong> and <strong>sale price</strong> per unit</p></li>
<li><p><strong>Supplier</strong> and <strong>lead time</strong>, useful for planning your purchasing</p></li>
</ul>
<p>Next, add a few formulas that do the work for you. With <strong>=current stock * cost price</strong> you calculate the stock value per line. A simple <strong>=IF(current stock &lt; minimum stock, "reorder", "ok")</strong> flags anything that's running low automatically. Add a row of <strong>SUM</strong> formulas at the top for total stock value and item count, and you'll have an overview at a glance.</p>

<h2>Filling in the template in 4 steps</h2>
<ol>
<li><p>Create one row per item and enter the SKU, name, and current stock.</p></li>
<li><p>Set your minimum stock based on your average sales and lead time, so you never run out.</p></li>
<li><p>Update the stock after every purchase and sale. Do this at a fixed moment, for example at the end of each day.</p></li>
<li><p>Filter weekly on the rows marked "reorder" and place your purchase orders per supplier.</p></li>
</ol>
<p>If several people work in the same file, use Google Sheets instead of a local Excel file. That keeps you from juggling conflicting versions.</p>
