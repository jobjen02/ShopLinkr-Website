<p>Want to <strong>connect Innosend to WooCommerce</strong> and automate your shipping? Innosend is a shipping platform and WooCommerce is a webshop platform. You connect them by adding your store as a sales channel, so orders come in and you can create shipping labels for them. In this article you will learn how that kind of connection works in general, and how <strong>ShopLinkr</strong> lets you handle your WooCommerce orders, inventory, and shipping from one system.</p>

<h2>How a shipping connection with WooCommerce works</h2>
<p>The idea behind every connection is the same: your shipping platform pulls orders from WooCommerce, you create a label for each one, and the track and trace goes back to the customer. In practice you almost always connect WooCommerce through an API key that you generate in your store and paste into the shipping platform. The steps look like this:</p>
<ol>
<li>In WooCommerce, go to <em>Settings, Advanced, REST API</em> and create a new key with read and write permissions.</li>
<li>Open your shipping platform and add WooCommerce as a new sales channel.</li>
<li>Enter your store details and the API key, then confirm the connection.</li>
<li>Place a test order to verify that it comes in and that the track and trace flows back correctly.</li>
</ol>
<p>If syncing does not work, it is almost always down to a wrong or expired API key, or permissions that are set too narrow. In that case, generate a new key and connect again.</p>
