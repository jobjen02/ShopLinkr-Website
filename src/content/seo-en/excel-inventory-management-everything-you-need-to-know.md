<p>For many new online stores, Excel inventory management is the first way to get a grip on stock. With a spreadsheet you track how much you have of each product, what it is worth, and when to reorder. It is free, flexible, and you probably already know the program. This article shows you how to set up a usable stock list, which formulas you need, and where Excel hits its limits once you start selling across more channels.</p>

<h2>Setting up an inventory list in Excel</h2>
<p>The foundation is a clear product list where every row is a single product. Keep it simple and consistent, and your file stays usable as it grows.</p>
<ol>
<li><p>Create a new spreadsheet with columns for product name, SKU, starting stock, purchase price, and selling price.</p></li>
<li><p>Add a <strong>stock value</strong> column with the formula <strong>=stock * purchase price</strong>, so you can immediately see how much capital is sitting in your warehouse.</p></li>
<li><p>Use conditional formatting to color rows red when stock drops below a minimum. That way you can see at a glance what needs reordering.</p></li>
<li><p>Apply data filters to the header row, so you can quickly sort by supplier, category, or stock level.</p></li>
</ol>
<p>With formulas like SUM, COUNT, and AVERAGE you can calculate total stock value and average margins. A pivot table then shows you which products move well and which are sitting still.</p>

<h2>Where Excel runs into limits</h2>
<p>As long as you sell through a single channel, a spreadsheet works fine. It gets tricky the moment you sell in several places at once. Excel does not work in real time, so you adjust stock by hand after every order. If you sell the same product on bol and in your own store, you risk selling something that is already gone.</p>
<p>On top of that, only one person can comfortably work in a file at a time, data entry errors are easy to miss, and there is no connection to your carrier or pick lists. The more orders you process, the more time it takes and the greater the chance of mistakes.</p>
