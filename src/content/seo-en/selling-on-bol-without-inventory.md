<p>Want to sell online but have no appetite for the hassle of inventory management? Then selling on bol without inventory might just be something for you. <h2>The concept of selling without inventory</h2>
<p>When you sell without inventory, it means you do not store or ship any physical products yourself. Instead, you work together with suppliers who send the products directly to your customers. As the seller, you are responsible for finding the right products and offering them on bol.com.</p>
<p>An important part of selling without inventory is the use of an automated system such as ShopLinkr. With this system you can connect carriers and sales channels to one another, so that you can process orders from a sales channel using the labels of a carrier.</p>
<h3>What does selling without inventory mean?</h3>
<p>With selling without inventory, you as the seller are not responsible for <a href="https://shoplinkr.com/en/blog/inventory-management">managing physical stock</a>. Instead, the inventory is managed by the suppliers.</p>
<p>When a customer orders a product from you, you make sure the order is passed on correctly to the supplier. The supplier then takes care of the delivery and you receive a commission on the sale.</p>
<h3>Advantages of selling without inventory</h3>
<p>Selling without inventory comes with several advantages. One of the biggest advantages is that you do not have to build up your own stock, and therefore have no costs for storage space. This not only saves money, but also time.</p>
<p>On top of that, you run no risk of unsellable products or surplus stock. You can always respond to the latest trends and offer popular products, without having to invest in them yourself.</p>
<p>Moreover, by eliminating inventory management activities, you can focus entirely on finding profitable products and optimizing your sales strategy.</p>
<p>Selling without inventory also offers opportunities for scalability. Because you are not limited to a particular stock, you can easily expand your assortment and offer more products to your customers. This allows you to grow your business without needing physical space for extra stock.</p>
<p>In addition, you can benefit from the expertise and experience of your suppliers. They often already have years of experience in delivering products and can give you valuable advice about which products sell well and which trends you can follow.</p>
<p>Selling without inventory also offers flexibility. You can quickly adapt your assortment to market demand and add new products as soon as they become popular. This allows you to always stay up to date and provide your customers with the newest and most sought-after products.</p>

<h2>Frequently asked questions about selling without inventory on bol</h2>
<h3>What about returns?</h3>
<p>As a seller without inventory, you are responsible for handling returns. Make sure you have a clear returns procedure and communicate this to your customers. Work together with your supplier to handle returns efficiently.</p>
<h3>What if a product is out of stock at the supplier?</h3>
<p>It can happen that a product is temporarily out of stock at the supplier. In that case it is important to inform the customer properly and to offer alternative options if possible. Communicate clearly with your supplier to make sure the stock levels are always up to date.</p>
<h2>The importance of customer service when selling without inventory</h2>
<h3>The importance of fast response times</h3>
<p>Fast response times are essential when selling without inventory. Customers expect their questions and problems to be resolved quickly. Make sure you are always reachable and respond to questions and complaints as quickly as possible.</p>
<h3>Building a good customer relationship</h3>
<p>A good customer relationship is crucial to the success of your sales activities. Invest in building trust and offer excellent customer service. Listen to your customers' feedback and take it into account when improving your products and service.</p>
<h2>The risks and challenges of selling without inventory</h2>
<h3>Managing supplier risks</h3>
<p>When selling without inventory, you run the risk that a supplier does not meet its obligations. That is why it is important to select suppliers carefully and make clear arrangements. Regularly monitor your suppliers' performance and step in when needed.</p>
<h3>Dealing with unexpected problems</h3>
<p>Selling without inventory can sometimes lead to unexpected problems, such as delays in deliveries or technical failures. It is important to be flexible and find solutions quickly. Communicate clearly to your customers and work together with your suppliers to solve problems.</p>
