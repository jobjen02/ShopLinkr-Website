<p>A customer finds exactly what they're looking for, clicks order, and it turns out to be sold out. Or worse: you sell the same product on bol and Shopify, and you sell one too many. Inventory management feels like a detail until it goes wrong. Then it costs you a sale, a review or a cancellation.</p>

<h2>What inventory management is really about</h2>
<p>Good inventory management is finding the right balance. Too much stock means capital tied up in your warehouse, plus the risk of slow-moving or outdated products. Too little stock means missed sales and disappointed customers. You want to know what you have, what sells quickly and what sits still, so you can adjust your purchasing accordingly.</p>
<p>As long as you sell on one channel, a spreadsheet still gets the job done. Sell on several channels at once and it gets tricky. Every sale on one channel has to be reflected on the others right away, otherwise you'll sell something that's no longer there.</p>
