<p>With ShopLinkr you can automatically receive an email as soon as the stock of a product drops below a set threshold. That way you are always notified in time and you avoid having to sell out of stock.</p>
<h3>How does it work?</h3>
<p>You set a minimum stock level at the company level. As soon as a product falls below that quantity, you automatically receive a notification by email. You decide for yourself whether you want to receive that notification right away, or once a day as a summary.</p>
<h3>Notification frequency</h3>
<p>When setting up stock notifications, you can choose between two options:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>Instant notification</strong>: you receive an email immediately as soon as a product drops below the set stock threshold.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Daily summary</strong>: every morning at 08:00 you receive a summary of all products whose stock has reached the lower limit.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<h3>Setting it up at the company level</h3>
<ol>
<li>
<p>Go to <strong>Settings &gt; My company</strong>.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Go to <strong>Stock notifications</strong>.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Set the default minimum stock and choose the notification frequency you want (instant or daily).</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p>These settings apply to all products, unless you override them per product.</p>
<h3>Adjusting per product</h3>
<p>Do you want to use a different stock threshold or notification frequency for a specific product? You can. Go to the product, click <strong>Edit</strong> and adjust the stock notification settings. These override the general company settings for this product only.</p>
<h3>Why use stock notifications?</h3>
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>Avoid selling out of stock</strong>: you know exactly when a product is almost gone, so you can step in on time.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Reorder from your supplier on time</strong>: you get an automatic heads-up as soon as reordering is needed.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Save time</strong>: no more manual checklists needed. Everything is tracked and reported automatically.</p>
</li>
</ul>
