<p>Every time a product's stock changes, it is recorded as a stock movement. That way you always have a complete overview of what happened to your stock and why.</p>
<h3>Where do you find stock movements?</h3>
<p>Go to <strong>Products</strong>, open a product and go to the <strong>Stock history</strong> tab. Here you see all movements for that product, from newest to oldest.</p>
<h3>What do you see per movement?</h3>
<p>The following information is shown for each movement:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>Reason</strong>: why the stock changed (see below)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Previous stock</strong>: the quantity before the change</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>New stock</strong>: the quantity after the change</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Adjustment</strong>: the difference (green for an increase, red for a decrease)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><a href="/en/support/location-management"><strong>Location</strong></a>: which warehouse location the change took place at</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Date</strong>: when the change took place</p>
</li>
</ul>
<h3>Types of movements</h3>
<p>The reason for a movement indicates what caused the stock to change. These are the types you may come across:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>Stock updated</strong>: a manual adjustment of the stock</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Picked</strong>: stock picked during the picking process</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Order processed</strong>: stock definitively written off after shipping</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Order cancelled</strong>: reserved stock released again</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Return</strong>: stock put back in stock after a <a href="/en/support/processing-a-return">return</a></p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Delivery received</strong>: stock added through a <a href="/en/support/receiving-and-processing-a-delivery">delivery</a> from a supplier</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Delivery reverted</strong>: the receipt of a delivery undone</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Moved</strong>: stock <a href="/en/support/move-stock-between-locations">moved</a> from one location to another</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Correction</strong>: an automatic correction by the system</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><a href="/en/support/stock-counts"><strong>Stock count</strong></a>: an adjustment after a stock count</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Stock synced</strong>: stock taken over from a sales channel (this happens once, when a product is first retrieved from your sales channel)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Product missing during picking</strong>: a stock adjustment because a product was not found during picking</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Wrong quantity during picking</strong>: a stock adjustment because a different quantity was found than expected</p>
</li>
</ul>
<h3>Why is this useful?</h3>
<p>Stock movements help you explain stock discrepancies. If a product's stock is not correct, the movements let you see exactly when and why the stock changed. That way you can quickly find out whether a mistake was made or whether there is another explanation.</p>
