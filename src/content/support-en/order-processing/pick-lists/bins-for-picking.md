<p>Bins let you organize orders during the pick process. Each bin gets a unique barcode, so while picking you know exactly which bin every product belongs in. When packing, you scan the bin and the right order opens automatically. This prevents mistakes and speeds up the packing process.</p>
<h3>Creating bins</h3>
<p>There are two ways to create bins:</p>
<h4>Manually</h4>
<ol>
<li>
<p>Go to <strong>Settings &gt; Bins</strong>.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Click <strong>Add bin</strong>.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Give the bin a <strong>name</strong> (it must be unique).</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Click <strong>Save</strong>.</p>
</li>
</ol>
<h4>Using the wizard</h4>
<p>The wizard lets you create several bins at once:</p>
<ol>
<li>
<p>Choose the <strong>type</strong>: letters (A, B, C...) or numbers (1, 2, 3...).</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Choose the <strong>starting value</strong> (for example "A" or "1").</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Choose the <strong>number</strong> of bins you want to create.</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p>The bins are created automatically with sequential names.</p>
<h3>Generating barcode labels</h3>
<p>Each bin can get a barcode label that you can print and stick on the physical bin. Click <strong>Generate label</strong> next to a bin. The barcode label opens, which you can print and stick on the bin.</p>
<h3>Using bins while picking</h3>
<p>When you pick with bins, it works as follows:</p>
<ol>
<li>
<p>ShopLinkr assigns each order to a specific bin.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>While picking, you first scan the <strong>product</strong> and then the <strong>bin</strong>.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>This way you can be sure that every product ends up in the right bin.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>When packing, you scan the bin and the right order opens automatically.</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p>See <a href="/en/support/working-with-pick-lists">Working with pick lists</a> for more information about the pick process with and without bins.</p>
