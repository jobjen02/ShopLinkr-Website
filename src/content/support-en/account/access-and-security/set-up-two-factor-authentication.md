<p>Two-factor authentication (2FA) adds an extra layer of security to your account. On top of your password, you'll also need a one-time code to log in. That keeps your account better protected, even if someone were to get hold of your password.</p>
<h3>What do you need?</h3>
<p>You'll need an authenticator app on your phone. Some suitable apps are:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Google Authenticator</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Microsoft Authenticator</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Authy</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>1Password</p>
</li>
</ul>
<h3>Enabling 2FA</h3>
<ol>
<li>
<p>Go to <strong>Settings &gt; Security</strong>.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>In the <strong>Two-factor authentication</strong> section, click <strong>Enable</strong>.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>A QR code appears. Scan it with your authenticator app.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Can't get the scan to work? You can also copy the secret key and enter it in your app manually.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Enter the 6-digit code shown by your app and click confirm.</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p>That's it! From now on you'll be asked for an extra code when logging in.</p>
<h3>How does logging in with 2FA work?</h3>
<p>After entering your email address and password, an extra step appears. Open your authenticator app, read off the 6-digit code, and enter it. The code changes every 30 seconds, so always use the most recent one.</p>
<h3>Disabling 2FA</h3>
<p>Want to turn 2FA off again? Go to <strong>Settings &gt; Security</strong> and click <strong>Disable</strong> next to Two-factor authentication. You'll need to enter a valid 6-digit code to confirm.</p>
